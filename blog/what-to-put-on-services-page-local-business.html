What to Put on a Services Page (Local Business Checklist)

By BUXI DIGITALDecember 15, 20258 min read

Your services page should help visitors self-select and take the next step. If it’s vague, they leave.

Quick Start

Add these three blocks: 1) A clear list of services in plain language
2) Proof (reviews, outcomes) 3) One CTA to Contact

The services page template

1) Who it’s for

Define your ideal customer and the problems you solve.

2) Service list (3–8 items)

Each service gets 2–3 bullets: what it includes, who it’s for, typical timeline.

3) “How it works”

A simple process reduces anxiety: request → consult → quote → deliver.

4) Proof

Add reviews and examples. Link to About if trust needs more context.

Action Box: services page checklist

- Plain-language service names - Clear “what’s included” bullets - Who it’s for (and not for) - Process section - Proof near the service list - One CTA repeated - Links to Blog for FAQs

Example scenario

A local business updates their services page with clear scopes and proof. Leads improve because visitors understand what they’re buying.
